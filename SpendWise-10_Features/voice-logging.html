How Voice Logging Works
1

One-Tap Mic Activation

User taps the mic button on the home screen or the floating widget. Designed for on-the-go capture accessible from any screen, even while driving or shopping.

2

Speech-to-Text Transcription

Google Speech API (Android) or Apple Speech (iOS) converts voice to text. The prototype uses the Web Speech API; the final version uses a model fine-tuned for Sri Lankan English accents.

3

NLP Entity Extraction

Same NLP engine as the SMS parser extracts amount, merchant name, and category. Time defaults to "now" unless the user says "yesterday" or a specific day name.

4

One-Tap Confirmation

Parsed result is shown in a confirm card. Any field can be tapped to edit. A single tap confirms and adds to the feed or one tap discards to re-record.

5

Added to Expense Feed

Voice entries join the same feed as SMS-parsed transactions treated identically in budget calculations. Tagged with a 🎙️ icon for transparency, but fully equivalent in reports.

Traceability

Implements FR-05 (voice‑based expense entry) and supports UC2 within the expense capture workflow. It is highlighted in the Competitive Advantage Summary as a key differentiator, as competing solutions such as Mint, FREMI, and Toshl provide no voice input support in their Sri Lanka configurations.

⚠ Prototype Limitation

The demo simulates voice by animating a preset transcript when a phrase chip is clicked. Full implementation requires the Google/Apple STT SDK, a fine-tuned Sri Lankan English accent model, and the NLP extraction pipeline. Sinhala/Tamil requires a separate STT model trained on local speech data.
