How the forecast works
SpendWise aggregates 90 days of anonymised SMS transaction data across opted-in users in a postcode. A time-series model (ARIMA + seasonal decomposition) identifies weekly demand cycles, public holiday spikes, and salary-day patterns. Retailers receive a 30-day forward forecast with a ±12% confidence interval enabling smarter stock ordering and promotion timing.

How the B2B intelligence model works
1

User Consent Layer (PDPA 2022)

Users see a clear opt-in during onboarding. Opted-in users are tagged for inclusion in aggregated datasets opting in is required and cannot be defaulted.

2

Anonymisation Pipeline

Individual transactions are aggregated at a minimum 200-transaction threshold. No personal identifiers are ever shared. Differential privacy algorithms applied to prevent re-identification.

3

AI Demand Forecasting

ARIMA + seasonal decomposition models analyse 90-day rolling windows to produce 30-day forward demand forecasts with confidence intervals telling retailers what will sell before it sells.

4

Retailer Dashboard Access

Retailers log into their SpendWise partner portal separate login tier. They see their catchment area demand index, competitor switching rates, heatmap, and AI forecast.

5

Campaign Creation & Delivery

Retailer creates a discount campaign in the portal. SpendWise targets users near that store with relevant category history delivering the offer as a pre-purchase alert (Page 01) before the user spends elsewhere.

Directly implements the Supplier-Oriented Marketplace B2B model: SpendWise as the sole provider of anonymised consumer intelligence accessed by multiple retail partners. Embeds the Porter Value Chain data feedback loop: retailers supply discount data → SpendWise delivers targeted alerts → users save → retailers gain footfall → stronger data → loop compounds. This is SpendWise's primary competitive moat. PDPA 2022 compliance is positioned as a competitive advantage, not a constraint. It enables partnerships that would be impossible for non-compliant competitors.
